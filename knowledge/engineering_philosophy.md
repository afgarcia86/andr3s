## My Approach to Building Software
I like building things quickly so we can learn from real systems instead of theoretical ones. My typical approach is to prototype early, validate the idea, and then refine the architecture once we understand the real constraints. I find this leads to better products and avoids over-engineering.

My background in design also influences how I build software. I care about how things feel to use, not just whether they technically work. Good systems should be understandable, predictable, and pleasant for both users and engineers.

## What "Well Done" Means
When I say something should be done well, I usually mean a few things:

- **Thoughtful user experience** — even highly technical systems should be intuitive and polished where users interact with them.
- **Simple, maintainable architecture** — I prefer systems that are easy to understand and extend over clever but fragile solutions.
- **Security by default** — systems should be designed with safe assumptions from the start rather than bolting security on later.

To me, good engineering balances practicality with long-term thinking. The goal isn’t perfection, it’s building systems that work reliably and can evolve over time.

## How I Work With Teams
I believe the best engineering happens in collaborative environments where people feel comfortable sharing ideas and challenging assumptions. I try to create an atmosphere where teammates feel encouraged and supported so everyone can do their best work.

One of my strengths is bridging technical teams and business or product partners. I enjoy translating complex technical ideas into something others can understand so teams can make better decisions together.

## Product Mindset
I’ve always been drawn to building real products used by real people. My industrial design background gave me an appreciation for the entire product experience, not just the code.

That perspective still shapes how I work today. I think about systems in terms of the whole product: how the hardware, software, and user experience come together to solve a real problem.

## Continuous Learning
I’m a self-taught engineer, and that experience shaped how I approach my career. I’m always trying to improve my skills and learn from the people around me.

Technology changes constantly, and I think the best engineers stay curious, humble, and willing to adapt.
