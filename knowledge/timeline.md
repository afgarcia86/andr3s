## Early Career — Industrial Design Foundations
I studied Industrial Design in Philadelphia, where I developed a strong appreciation for thoughtful product design and user experience. My goal at the time was to design automobiles, inspired by designers like Raymond Loewy. This background gave me a product-first mindset that still influences how I approach engineering today.

## First Professional Role — Jewelry Design & My First Website
My first job out of school was with a small family-owned jewelry design company. I created 3D models based on hand-drawn sketches that were later used to produce molds for jewelry manufacturing. When the company needed a website and marketing materials, I volunteered to build them myself. That first website sparked my interest in software development.

## Freelance Years — Learning to Build on My Own
After the 2008 financial crisis impacted the company, I began freelancing and learning web development more seriously. I picked up projects through Craigslist and freelance platforms, building websites and experimenting with new technologies. This period taught me how to learn quickly, solve problems independently, and ship real work for real clients.

## Startup Experience — Yunno & Dreamit Accelerator
I joined a startup called Yunno that was accepted into the Dreamit Venture Accelerator program. During this time I worked closely with experienced engineers who helped sharpen my coding practices and software design thinking. After the program, I was invited to return to Dreamit as a Designer-in-Residence where I helped multiple startups with product design and technical direction.

## Building My Own Studio — Andres The Giant
While continuing to grow my engineering skills, I formally launched my own LLC called **Andres The Giant**. I built custom WordPress themes and websites for a variety of brands, combining my design background with my growing technical abilities. This period helped me become comfortable building complete products from scratch.

## Startup Growth — SnipSnap
One of the startups I had worked with through Dreamit, SnipSnap, later invited me to join their team. I initially focused on frontend development and product experience as the company grew. This role exposed me to the challenges of scaling a startup product and working with emerging technologies like computer vision.

## Acquisition & Platform Expansion — Slyce
SnipSnap was eventually acquired by Slyce, where my role evolved significantly. I transitioned into backend systems and solutions engineering, eventually becoming Director of Solutions Engineering. I worked closely with enterprise partners to design and implement computer vision-powered retail solutions.

## Enterprise Partnerships — stratis IoT
I joined stratis IoT as Lead Partner Solutions Engineer, where I worked with major technology partners integrating the stratis smart-home platform. This role strengthened my confidence as a self-taught engineer and highlighted one of my strongest skills: bridging the gap between engineering teams and strategic partners.

## Building Teams — RealPage
After stratis was acquired by RealPage, I joined the RealPage engineering organization and helped build a Partner Engineering team. I managed three engineers and oversaw integrations between stratis technology, internal RealPage systems, and external partners.

## Present — Lead Engineer at Harbor
Today I serve as Lead Engineer at Harbor, where I joined as the first engineering hire. I’ve helped turn an early concept into a real product used by parents to reduce stress and get better rest. My work spans device software, backend systems, AI-powered insights, and product architecture.

## What's Next
I’m excited by opportunities to build meaningful products that combine software, hardware, and AI. I’m especially drawn to environments where I can help take ambitious ideas from early concept to real-world impact while working with talented, collaborative teams.
